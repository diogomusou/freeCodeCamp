Miyamoto Musashi
My greatest influence to become a better person

Miyamoto Musashi (Japan, 1584-1645) was an expert swordsman and rōnin that became famous for his many undefeated duels and for innovating Japanese swordsmanship. He invented the style of simultaneously wielding both the katana and the wakizashi (Niten Ichi-ryū), something unheard of at that time in Japanese history. Musashi was a master of kenjutsu, strategy and bushidō ("the way of the warrior"), but by applying his insight and knowledge, he also became an accomplished artist, sculptor, calligrapher and writer.

"From one thing, know ten thousand things."

Miyamoto Musashi in The Book of Five Rings

Since Musashi’s time there have been many fictional texts about him, so it is difficult to verify the details about his life and separate fact from fiction. Currently, the most famous version is from Eiji Yoshikawa’s novel (1930), which has influenced most of the movies and mangas about Musashi that we can see today. In this novel, Musashi starts as a rather brutal and over-eager teenager who just wants to defeat everyone and become known as the greatest swordsman. However, in his search for power he is taught many things about bushidō and buddhism, and little by little becomes a man who aspires to true enlightenment by conquering himself.

"Today is victory over yourself of yesterday."

Miyamoto Musashi in The Book of Five Rings

Miyamoto Musashi is a symbol of Japanese principles and many people have been influenced by his life. I hope that his teachings will reach many more, not only in Japan but all over the world, and continue to motivate us to become a better person everyday.
